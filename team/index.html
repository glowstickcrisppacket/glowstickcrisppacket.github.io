Team

Glow Stick Crisp Packet is a co-creation, and many people have been involved over the various iterations of the idea.

From it's beginnings at the festival where the first discovery was made, through various evenings of creation and experimentation (Crisp Packet HQ meetings), our collaborators at events such as Rumpus, Fabulus of Unicorns, Decompression, with Nest TV and Laser Porn, through to participants who have got involved at events and festivals, and like minded individuals who have found us through our installations and performances.

Daz - After the initial experiments with glow sticks and crisp packets, Daz has pushed development of the idea of the Glow Stick Crisp Packet. Daz has experience building custom LED fixtures which have featured in parties, festivals and clubs, including working with Halo and Superbros to build custom LED fixtures and video projection mapping for the Bunker stage at Global Gathering festival.

Dave - co-creator and creator of the first glow stick crisp packet installation big enough to climb inside.

Michelle - originator and (accidental) creator of the worlds first glow stick crisp packet.

Matt - has extensive experience building interactive artworks, through previous experience working for Jason Bruge Studio, Halo, Superbros. Recent projects include the Qube nightclub in London, an installation featuring video projection mapping and custom LEDs.

Adam - trained as an industrial designer but works on a wide range of projects doing visualisation, content creation and design/development of digital interactive art installations.

Michael - As the founder of Natural Born Storytellers, Michael has experience curating true storytelling shows and workshops across London and Shambala festival.

Nikki - co-creator, performer and coach. Nikki inspired and developed the portable crisp packet kit and started the inspiring Community of Opportunity project.

Rob - was lead choreographer and conductor at the ‘Peace One Day’ performances in Trafalgar Square. Concert appearances include Go Local, working again alongside director Kim Gavin, leading choreography for 100 drummers on the O2 Wireless Festival Main stage. Rob has also compared the cabaret and main stage at London Burning Man Decompression since 2009.
